Essential Blood Donation Guidelines

Mandatory Recovery Intervals (Cooldowns)

To protect donor health and ensure a high-quality blood supply, the platform enforces strict, gender-specific intervals based on international best practices.

  1. Men: You must wait a minimum of 90 days (approx. 12 weeks) between donations to ensure full recovery.
  2. Women: You must wait a minimum of 120 days (approx. 16 weeks) between donations, acknowledging the need for longer iron level recovery.
  3. Automated Safety: You will be ineligible for scheduling or SOS alerts until your specific cooldown period has fully elapsed.

Health & Screening Criteria

While the platform manages logistics, the physical donation is subject to clinical safety standards:

  1. Pre-Donation Screening: Every donor must undergo a standard offline health screening at the blood bank or hospital before the donation takes place.
  2. Eligibility Awareness: Potential donors should be aware of barriers like fear or illness that may temporarily prevent donation.
  3. Voluntary Focus: We prioritize "Voluntary Non-Remunerated Donors" over "Replacement Donors" (family/friends) to ensure a more stable and safe national supply.

Operational Expectations

  1. Validation via QR: Your donation is only officially recorded when a technician scans your unique, HMAC-secured QR code.
  2. Double-Entry Verification: For security, the blood bank must also manually confirm the receipt of the blood unit on their dashboard.
  3. Geo-Fencing for Emergencies: If you respond to an SOS request, you should ideally be within a 10km radius of the requesting hospital to ensure timely delivery.

Data Privacy & Ethics

  1. No Medical Records: The system explicitly does not store sensitive personal health information (PHI) or medical records.
  2. Data Minimization: We only track metadata related to the donation event (who, where, and when) to maintain your privacy.
  3. Transparency: Donors have the right to view or delete their logistical data at any time.

The "Why" (Impact Awareness)

  1. Target Benchmark: Nepal needs 1% of its population (~320,000 units) to donate annually to meet basic needs.
  2. Addressing the Chasm: Your donation helps bridge the 90% daily deficit often seen during festival seasons and dengue outbreaks.
  3. Reducing Wait Times By following these criteria, you help reduce the current median transfusion delay of 2 hours and 15 minutes
